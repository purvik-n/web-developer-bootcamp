Chickens (Gallus gallus domesticus)

About this project | Read on Wikipedia

Overview

The chicken (Gallus gallus domesticus) is a domesticated form of the red junglefowl (Gallus gallus), originally native to Southeast Asia. It was first domesticated around 8,000 years ago and is one of the most common and widespread domesticated animals in the world. Chickens are primarily kept for their meat and eggs, though they are also kept as pets.

As of 2023, the global chicken population exceeds 26.5 billion, with more than 50 billion birds produced annually for consumption. Their economic importance makes them a central component of global animal husbandry.

Phylogeny

Chickens belong to the order Galliformes and the family Phasianidae. Their closest wild relative is the red junglefowl, and genetic studies confirm that the domestic chicken descended primarily from this species, with possible hybridization from other junglefowl species.

Domestication

Origins in Austronesia

Archaeological and genetic evidence suggests that chickens were first domesticated in Southeast Asia, with significant spread facilitated by Austronesian peoples across the Pacific. Remains of domesticated chickens have been found in sites dating back to roughly 6000 BCE in China.

Key Milestones in Domestication

  1. Initial domestication in Southeast Asia (~6000 BCE)
  2. Spread to South Asia and China via trade routes
  3. Introduction to the Middle East and Africa (~2500 BCE)
  4. Arrival in Europe via Greece and Rome (~800 BCE)
  5. Global spread through European colonization (15th–17th century)

Breeds of Chickens

There are over 500 recognized breeds of chicken, each suited for different purposes.

  • Egg-Laying Breeds
    • Leghorn – prolific white-egg layer
    • Rhode Island Red – hardy and productive
    • Sussex – dual-purpose, good layers
  • Meat Breeds
    • Cornish Cross – standard broiler breed
    • Jersey Giant – one of the largest breeds
  • Ornamental & Bantam Breeds
    • Silkie – fluffy, docile, great pets
    • Polish – distinctive feathered crest
    • Bantam – miniature versions of standard breeds

Key Facts

Lifespan
5–10 years (wild); 3–7 years (farmed)
Eggs per year
Up to 300+ eggs (high-production breeds)
Communication
Chickens use over 30 distinct vocalizations to communicate.
Vision
They can see in color and have a wider field of vision than humans.
